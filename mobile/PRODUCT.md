# ReelFeel — Product Context

## Product Purpose
Personal film and TV tracking app. Users log what they've watched, rate it, attach emotions, write short reviews, and build a watchlist. A discovery feed surfaces trending, now-playing, and personalised recommendations based on viewing history.

## Register
product

## Users
Film and TV enthusiasts who want a personal record of what they've watched — not social sharing, not Letterboxd clone. The primary use moment is post-watch: you finish a film, you open the app, you log it before the feeling fades. Secondary use is pre-watch discovery: browsing the recommendation feed or searching before deciding what to watch.

## Brand & Tone
Brutalist: heavy borders (2px solid), hard box shadows (no blur), monospace accents, gold + red as the only two chromatic accents on a near-black ground. Conversational copy ("what are we watching? 👀", "fetching the good stuff 🍿"). Irreverent but not juvenile. The design should feel like a zine about cinema, not a SaaS dashboard.

## Anti-references
- Letterboxd (soft serif, pastel washes, social graph emphasis)
- Standard SaaS blue-and-white dashboards
- App Store "polished" feel with rounded gradients and glassmorphism
- Pure dark-mode-as-aesthetic (dark for dark's sake with no intentional palette)

## Strategic Principles
1. Speed of logging is paramount — post-watch friction must be zero.
2. Emotion tagging is a differentiator; it should feel expressive, not clinical.
3. Recommendations are personal, not algorithmic-generic. "Why" labels matter.
4. Light mode and dark mode are first-class. Brutalist works in both.
